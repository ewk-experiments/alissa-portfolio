MySwimPro App Onboarding

Improved the onboarding experience for the #1 swim training app on the Apple Store, increasing users' likelihood to subscribe to premium by 21%.

Role

UX Designer

Skills

ResearchPrototypingVisual Design

Company

MySwimPro

Overview

MySwimPro is the #1 swim training app on the Apple Store. The onboarding experience was a critical conversion point -- the moment where new users either saw the value of premium features or dropped off. The goal was to redesign onboarding to increase the likelihood of premium subscription.

+21%
Increase in premium subscription likelihood

Research

Conducted extensive research to understand user motivations, pain points, and expectations during the onboarding flow.

114
Survey respondents
6
In-depth interviews

The survey and interview data revealed key insights about what users valued most, what confused them during onboarding, and what information they needed to make a subscription decision.

Personas

Synthesized research findings into personas that captured the different user types and their unique needs during onboarding. These personas guided design decisions about what information to present, in what order, and how to communicate the value of premium features to different audiences.

User personas based on survey and interview research

Design Iterations

Iteration 1

Redesigned the onboarding flow based on research insights. Focused on communicating premium value earlier and reducing friction in the signup process. Tested with users to identify remaining pain points.

Iteration 2 (Final)

Refined the flow based on first-round testing feedback. Improved the progression of information, visual design of premium feature previews, and clarity of the subscription decision point.

MySwimPro onboarding flow - before and after redesign

Validation

The final design was validated through multivariate testing, comparing the redesigned onboarding flow against the original. The test measured premium subscription likelihood as the primary success metric, with secondary metrics including onboarding completion rate and time to first workout.

Outcome

The redesigned onboarding experience achieved a 21% increase in users' likelihood to subscribe to premium. The research-driven approach -- starting with 114 survey respondents and 6 in-depth interviews, then validating through multivariate testing -- ensured the design decisions were grounded in real user needs rather than assumptions.

Upstage Arts Website Redesign

Redesigned the desktop and mobile website experience for a non-profit theater company in Texas, contributing to a 60% increase in mobile traffic after release.

Role

UX/UI Designer

Skills

Information ArchitectureVisual DesignUX Writing

Client

Upstage Arts (Non-profit theater, Texas)

Overview

Upstage Arts, a non-profit theater company in Texas, needed a website that better served their audience across devices. The existing site was outdated, difficult to navigate on mobile, and didn't effectively communicate their programming and mission.

+60%
Increase in mobile traffic after redesign

Content Analysis

Started with a thorough content analysis of the existing site to understand what information was most important to visitors and where the current site was falling short. This revealed opportunities to restructure the information architecture and improve content hierarchy for both desktop and mobile experiences.

Wix Migration

The redesign included migrating the site to Wix, giving the Upstage Arts team the ability to manage and update content independently. The platform choice was driven by the client's need for a user-friendly CMS that their non-technical staff could maintain.

Iterative Design

Iteration 1

Initial wireframes and layout exploration based on content analysis findings. Focused on establishing clear information hierarchy.

Iteration 2

Visual design development incorporating Upstage Arts branding. Focused on mobile-first responsive layout.

Iteration 3 (Final)

Refined design based on client feedback and testing. Polished responsive behavior, typography, and visual polish for launch.

Client Testimonial

"The new website has been a game-changer for us. We're seeing significantly more engagement from mobile users, and our team can actually update the site ourselves now."

-- Upstage Arts

Outcome

The redesigned site launched on Wix with a mobile-responsive design that contributed to a 60% increase in mobile traffic. The new information architecture made it easier for visitors to discover shows, learn about the organization, and get involved.

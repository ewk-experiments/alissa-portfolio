University of Michigan Landing Page Redesign

Redesigned landing pages for University of Michigan campus computers, increasing usage by 10% through research-driven iterative design.

Role

UX Designer

Skills

ResearchVisual DesignInformation Architecture

Organization

University of Michigan

Overview

The University of Michigan campus computers used a default browser landing page that wasn't serving students effectively. The goal was to redesign this landing page to increase engagement and provide students with quick access to the resources they need most.

+10%
Increase in landing page usage

Research

Conducted research to understand how students used campus computers and what resources they needed most frequently. Methods included:

  • Observational studies of students using campus computer labs
  • Surveys on most-accessed university resources
  • Analysis of existing landing page analytics
  • Competitive analysis of peer institution approaches

Design Inspiration

Drew inspiration from two key sources: the existing University of Michigan ITS (Information Technology Services) website for visual language and branding consistency, and the Momentum Chrome extension for its approach to creating a useful, visually engaging new-tab experience.

Design inspiration: ITS website and Momentum Chrome extension

Iterative Design

Iteration 1

Initial exploration focused on surfacing the most-used university resources. Established the basic layout and information hierarchy.

Iteration 2

Refined the visual design based on early feedback. Improved the balance between branding and utility.

Iteration 3

Incorporated user feedback from testing sessions. Adjusted the information architecture to better match student mental models.

Iteration 4 (Final)

Polished visual design with strong University of Michigan branding, quick-access resource links, and a clean layout that loads fast on campus machines.

Final landing page design for campus computers

Outcome

The redesigned landing page achieved a 10% increase in usage, indicating that students were engaging with the page rather than immediately navigating away. The design balanced university branding with practical utility, giving students fast access to the tools and resources they use most.
